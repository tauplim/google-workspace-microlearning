# Strategic Context to Course Design: Handover Bridge

## Purpose

This section bridges the `industry-research-expert` agent's strategic context document to the `adult-training-course-writer` agent's course design decisions. It transforms market research into actionable design parameters without prescribing specific structures.

---

## Section A: Extract Target Learner Profile

From the strategic context document, extract:

- **Primary learner role** - Who will take this course?
- **Experience level** - Beginner, intermediate, or advanced?
- **Time constraints** - Hours per year available for training?
- **Pain points** - What problems does this course solve for them?
- **Success definition** - What does "good outcome" look like for learner?

---

## Section B: Extract Market Positioning

- **Opportunity gap** - What is missing from current market offerings?
- **Differentiation** - What makes this course unique vs competitors?
- **Competitor context** - Who are the primary competitors and their weaknesses?

---

## Section C: Extract Format Constraints

- **Content format** - Microlearning, video, hands-on, etc.?
- **Session length** - Typical learner attention/focus window?
- **Learning hour allocation** - How much time will target learner spend?
- **Delivery mode** - Self-paced, instructor-led, hybrid?

---

## Section D: Design Implications Mapping

For each key research finding, document the corresponding design implication.

| Research Finding | Design Implication |
|------------------|-------------------|
| [Market opportunity] | [How does this affect course scope?] |
| [Target learner pain point] | [How does this shape content priority?] |
| [Competitor gap] | [How does this create differentiation?] |
| [Format constraint] | [How does this limit or enable design?] |
| [Pricing context] | [How does this affect course tier/freemium?] |

---

## Section E: Success Metrics Definition

Define what successful learning looks like:

- **Immediate metric** - What can learner do after one session/module?
- **Short-term metric** - What improvement expected within 30 days?
- **Long-term metric** - How does this course affect business outcomes?

---

## Section F: Module Priority Guidance

Based on competitive gap and learner pain points, suggest which topics deserve:

- **Highest priority** - Core value proposition, must-have skills
- **Secondary priority** - Important but not urgent
- **Tertiary priority** - Nice-to-have, advanced topics
- **Exclusions** - What the course will NOT cover (scope boundary)

---

## Section G: Differentiation Focus

Identify the 2-3 key things this course must do well to stand out:

1. [Primary differentiator] - [Why learner should choose this over alternatives]
2. [Secondary differentiator] - [Supporting unique value]
3. [Tertiary differentiator] - [Nice-to-have edge]

---

## Usage Notes

This bridge section is **topic-agnostic** and can be inserted into any course preparation prompt where a strategic context document is the input. The course writer uses this bridge to make informed decisions without being constrained by pre-specified structures.

The course writer retains full authority over:
- Number of modules
- Module length
- Pedagogical approach
- Slide design
- Activity selection
